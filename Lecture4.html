Lecture 4 – The Gulfstream

Vorticity Equation

Our full vorticity equation rewritten with \(\psi\) is: \[ \overbrace{ \underbrace{\frac{\partial}{\partial t} (\nabla^2 \psi)}_{\text{Local change of relative vorticity } \zeta = \frac{\partial v}{\partial x} - \frac{\partial u}{\partial y}} + \underbrace{J(\psi, \nabla^2 \psi)}_{\text{Jacobian, Nonlinear advection of vorticity}} }^{\text{Material derivative } \frac{D\zeta}{Dt}} + \underbrace{\beta \psi_x}_{\text{Meridional advection of planetary vorticity}} = \underbrace{\frac{f_0}{D} w_E}_{\text{Column Stretching/compression by Ekman pumping}} - \underbrace{r \nabla^2 \psi}_{\text{Linear bottom friction}} + \underbrace{k_H \nabla^4 \psi}_{\text{Horizontal friction}} \] with \[J(A, B) = A_x B_y - A_y B_x\]
